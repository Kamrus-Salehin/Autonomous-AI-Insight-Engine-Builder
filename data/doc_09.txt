Famous Renaissance Artists and Their Works
The Renaissance (roughly 14th to 17th century) was a period of intense artistic, cultural, and intellectual rebirth in Europe, following the Middle Ages. Italy was its epicenter, producing some of the most iconic figures and masterpieces in art history.

Key Renaissance Artists:

Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519):

Known for: A true "Renaissance Man," excelling not only as a painter but also as a sculptor, architect, musician, scientist, inventor, anatomist, geologist, cartographer, botanist, and writer. His scientific curiosity deeply informed his art.

Notable Works:

Mona Lisa (c. 1503-1519): Perhaps the most famous painting in the world, renowned for its enigmatic smile, sfumato technique (soft, hazy transitions between colors and tones), and psychological depth. Located in the Louvre Museum, Paris.

The Last Supper (c. 1495-1498): A monumental fresco depicting the final meal of Jesus with his apostles. Famous for its dramatic composition, emotional intensity, and innovative use of perspective. Located in the Santa Maria delle Grazie convent, Milan.

Vitruvian Man (c. 1490): A drawing accompanied by notes based on the ideal human proportions described by the ancient Roman architect Vitruvius. It symbolizes the blend of art and science.

Michelangelo di Lodovico Buonarroti Simoni (1475–1564):

Known for: A towering figure of the High Renaissance, primarily as a sculptor, but also as a painter, architect, and poet. He believed the form was already present within the stone, waiting to be "released."

Notable Works:

David (1501-1504): A colossal marble statue depicting the biblical hero David before his battle with Goliath. Celebrated for its anatomical precision and powerful emotional expression. Located in the Galleria dell'Accademia, Florence.

Sistine Chapel Ceiling (1508-1512): A monumental fresco cycle covering the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel in the Vatican City, depicting scenes from the Book of Genesis, most famously "The Creation of Adam." Known for its immense scale, dramatic figures, and vibrant colors.

Pietà (1498-1499): A marble sculpture depicting the Virgin Mary cradling the dead body of Jesus after his crucifixion. Renowned for its exquisite craftsmanship, emotional tenderness, and idealized beauty. Located in St. Peter's Basilica, Vatican City.

Raffaello Sanzio da Urbino (Raphael) (1483–1520):

Known for: Master of painting and architecture of the High Renaissance. Celebrated for the clarity of form, ease of composition, and visual splendor of his Neoplatonic ideals of human grandeur.

Notable Works:

The School of Athens (c. 1509-1511): A grand fresco in the Apostolic Palace of the Vatican, depicting a gathering of ancient Greek philosophers and scientists. Famous for its perfect perspective, harmonious composition, and intellectual depth.

Sistine Madonna (c. 1512): A oil painting depicting the Madonna and Child flanked by Saint Sixtus II and Saint Barbara, with two iconic cherubs at the bottom. Known for its serene beauty and spiritual quality. Located in the Gemäldegalerie Alte Meister, Dresden.

Transfiguration (1517-1520): Raphael's last painting, showcasing his mastery of dramatic composition, light, and emotion, depicting two distinct but interconnected biblical narratives.
