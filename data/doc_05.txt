Key Events of World War II
World War II was a global war that lasted from 1939 to 1945. It involved the vast majority of the world's countries, including all the great powers, forming two opposing military alliances: the Allies and the Axis. It was the deadliest conflict in human history, resulting in an estimated 70 to 85 million fatalities.

Major Key Events:

Invasion of Poland (September 1, 1939): Germany invades Poland, using "Blitzkrieg" (lightning war) tactics. This event marks the official beginning of WWII as Britain and France declare war on Germany.

Battle of Britain (July-October 1940): A sustained aerial bombardment campaign by the German Luftwaffe against the United Kingdom. The Royal Air Force (RAF) successfully defended Britain, preventing a planned German invasion.

Operation Barbarossa (June 22, 1941): Germany launches a massive surprise invasion of the Soviet Union, opening the Eastern Front, the largest land theater of war in history.

Attack on Pearl Harbor (December 7, 1941): Imperial Japan launches a surprise attack on the U.S. naval base at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii. This event prompts the United States to declare war on Japan and enter WWII.

Battle of Stalingrad (August 1942 - February 1943): A brutal and decisive battle on the Eastern Front. The Soviet Union's victory against the German Sixth Army marked a major turning point, shifting the momentum in favor of the Allies.

D-Day (Normandy Landings - June 6, 1944): Allied forces launch the largest seaborne invasion in history, landing on the beaches of Normandy, France. This opens a crucial second front in Western Europe against Nazi Germany.

Battle of the Bulge (December 1944 - January 1945): Germany's last major offensive on the Western Front. Allied forces eventually repel the attack, exhausting Germany's remaining resources.

V-E Day (Victory in Europe Day - May 8, 1945): Germany formally surrenders to the Allies, marking the end of the war in Europe.

Atomic Bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki (August 6 & 9, 1945): The United States drops atomic bombs on the Japanese cities of Hiroshima and Nagasaki, leading to immense destruction and loss of life.

V-J Day (Victory over Japan Day - September 15, 1945 - formal signing): Japan formally surrenders, signing the Instrument of Surrender aboard the USS Missouri, officially ending World War II. (Note: Initial surrender announced August 15).
