The Seven Wonders of the Ancient World
The Seven Wonders of the Ancient World are a list of remarkable constructions of classical antiquity, compiled by ancient Hellenic travelers. Only one of these wonders still stands today.

The Great Pyramid of Giza (Egypt):

Location: Giza, Egypt.

Built for: Pharaoh Khufu (Cheops) of the Fourth Dynasty.

Construction: Circa 2580–2560 BC.

Description: The largest of the three pyramids at the Giza necropolis, it was the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 3,800 years. It is the only ancient wonder still largely intact.

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon (Mesopotamia):

Location: Ancient city of Babylon, near modern-day Hillah, Babil province, Iraq.

Built by: Nebuchadnezzar II for his homesick wife, Amytis (disputed by some historians).

Construction: Circa 600 BC.

Description: A legendary terraced garden structure with various trees, shrubs, and flowers. Its existence is debated among historians due to a lack of definitive archaeological evidence.

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia (Greece):

Location: Olympia, Greece (within the Temple of Zeus).

Built by: Greek sculptor Phidias.

Construction: Circa 435 BC.

Description: A massive seated figure, about 13 meters (43 ft) tall, made of ivory plates and gold panels over a wooden framework. It depicted the god Zeus. It was destroyed in the 5th century AD.

The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus (Anatolia/Turkey):

Location: Ephesus (near modern-day Selçuk in Turkey).

Built for: The Greek goddess of the hunt, Artemis.

Construction: Several phases, the most famous rebuilt around 550 BC and later in 323 BC.

Description: A magnificent Greek temple, famous for its size and intricate sculptures. It was destroyed by arson and later by invading Goths.

The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus (Anatolia/Turkey):

Location: Halicarnassus (modern-day Bodrum, Turkey).

Built for: Mausolus, a satrap in the Persian Empire, and his wife Artemisia II.

Construction: Circa 353–350 BC.

Description: An elaborate tomb, so grand that it gave us the word "mausoleum." It was destroyed by a series of earthquakes between the 12th and 15th centuries AD.

The Colossus of Rhodes (Greece):

Location: Harbor of Rhodes, Greek island of Rhodes.

Built for: The sun god Helios.

Construction: Between 292 and 280 BC.

Description: A colossal bronze statue of the sun god Helios, standing over 30 meters (100 ft) high, straddling the harbor entrance. It stood for only 54 years before being toppled by an earthquake.

The Lighthouse of Alexandria (Egypt):

Location: Pharos island, Alexandria, Egypt.

Built by: Ptolemaic Kingdom.

Construction: Circa 280–247 BC.

Description: One of the tallest man-made structures of its time, estimated to be over 100 meters (330 ft) high. Its light was said to be visible from up to 35 miles away. It was severely damaged by earthquakes and its ruins were finally destroyed in 1480 AD.
